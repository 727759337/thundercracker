
Note - it's recommended to build out of tree. For LLVM, for example:
 * unzip llvm-3.0-src
 * mkdir llvm-3.0-build at a sibling level to llvm-3.0-src
 * cd into llvm-3.0-build
 * ../llvm-3.0-src/configure <flags as per below>
 * make -j4

Build-time configuration for Clang and LLVM in deps:

--enable-targets=arm --target=arm --enable-optimized --enable-static --disable-jit --disable-docs --disable-threads --disable-pthreads --disable-pic --disable-libffi

Debug build config:

--enable-targets=arm --target=arm --disable-optimized --enable-static --enable-assertions --disable-jit --disable-docs --disable-threads --disable-pthreads --disable-pic --disable-libffi

XXX: on Windows, append LDFLAGS=-static to the configure line to avoid a very slow clang: http://lists.cs.uiuc.edu/pipermail/cfe-dev/2012-April/020558.html

XXX: on Windows with MinGW-64 (used in order to get a more recent GCC version), I needed to patch lib/Support/Errno.cpp to pass 'MaxErrStrLen-1' as the third param to strerror_s()

XXX: On the Linux build, this failed while trying to link libclang.so (due to lack of -fPIC) but that's okay... we didn't ask for nor need a shared lib.
